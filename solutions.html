Possible Solutions

  • Get residents to volunteer to clean: offer community service credit to younger residents

    • New York Cares is an organization that coordinates volunteers around NYC to help residents in need. This organization could aid with the effort of cleaning street litter as it has lead such efforts on a smaller scale
    • New York City High Schools require community service as a part of their graduation requirements and curriculum. Many students are willing to help because they need to fulfill this requirement. Students would be a great asset in this effort. This is a great way to get the neighborhoods clean for no cost at all
  • Assign cleaning crews to areas in need
    • Neighborhoods like Pelham Parkway, Marble Hill, and Washington Heights are riddled with litter that is seldom collected, but there are dedicated cleaning crews for affluent neighborhoods in Manhattan, south of 110th Street
    • If money could be raised or collected to dedicate teams to clean target neighborhoods, perhaps more people would visit that area and increase the economic development, as more cash flows though the neighborhood.


  • Provide incentives for clean neighborhoods participating in the clean up initiative
    • Once one of the aforementioned strategies are implemented, neighborhoods and individuals should be rewarded for their efforts.
    • Community leaders and volunteers should be recognized for their actions in the form of awards and other recognition from local governments. People enjoy being recognized by others, and this will only further incentivize the clean up initiative
