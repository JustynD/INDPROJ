Clean our Streets!

There is a huge problem with garbage piling up in our streets in New York, everywhere you look, you will see trash piling on on the sidewalk. But the true problem with this is that one will not see such trash in downtown Manhattan, but in upper Manhattan and outer boroughs. There are dedicated clean up teams for Lower Manhattan but not many for other neighborhoods.

This issue creates a hazardous environment for residents and lowers the quality and standard of living. This keeps tourists away from locations other than Lower Manhattan, leading to a lack of economic development in these neighborhoods as there is no extra influx of money to local small businesses. Along with this, even residents of other neighborhoods in the city do not travel to these areas as they hold a reputation for being dirty.

Lower income neighborhoods are perpetually stuck in this cycle as local governments can not afford, nor do they plan to designate cleaning teams to the extent that affluent neighborhoods can. Local government in New York City runs with the stigma against outer borough neighborhoods which leads to the complete exclusion of such issues in any government attended initiatives
